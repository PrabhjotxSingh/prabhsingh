
Lakota West Class of 2011: Football

<p>
    As a junior at Lakota West in 2009, Ryan Kelly was a first team
    all-conference and all-city selection on the offensive line. During his
    senior season, he suffered a torn ACL in the last game of his season.
    Although selected, he was unable to play at the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Under_Armour_All-America_Game"
        title="Under Armour All-America Game"
    >
        Under Armour All-America Game
    </a>
    <u>c</u>
oming off his knee injury. Rated as a three-star recruit by    <em><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a></em>
    , Ryan was ranked as the No. 4 center prospect in his class.
</p>
<p>
    After
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redshirt_(college_sports)"
        title="Redshirt (college sports)"
    >
        redshirting
    </a>
    his initial year in Tuscaloosa, Ryan came off the bench to play in 10 games
    of the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2012_Alabama_Crimson_Tide_football_team"
        title="2012 Alabama Crimson Tide football team"
    >
        2012 season
    </a>
    at center, backing up All-American senior
    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barrett_Jones" title="Barrett Jones">
        Barrett Jones
    </a>
    at Alabama. Ryan was named to the SEC All-Freshman team. In his sophomore
    year, Ryan took over starting duties. As a junior, Ryan was named to the
    Rimington Trophy watch list and started 12 games at center. A highly
    reliable anchor on the offensive line, he missed just seven assignments in
    806 snaps on the year for a 99.1 percent success rate and did not allow a
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quarterback_sack"
        title="Quarterback sack"
    >
        quarterback sack
    </a>
    . In his senior season, Ryan missed only eight assignments in 1,012 snaps
    for a success rate of 99.2 percent on the season, and committed just one
    penalty in 1,012 snaps with no holding calls. He was a consensus
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_College_Football_All-America_Team"
        title="2015 College Football All-America Team"
    >
        first team All-America
    </a>
    selection, earning first-team honors from Walter Camp, USA Today, Sporting
    News, the FWAA, and the AFCA, and won the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rimington_Trophy"
        title="Rimington Trophy"
    >
        Rimington Trophy
    </a>
    in 2015. He was a member of Alabama's BCS National Championships in 2011
    and 2012 and the College Football Playoff National Championship in 2015.
Ryan earned his bachelor's and Master's degree in marketing from Alabama.    <sup></sup>
</p>

    The
    <a

    </a>

        Jeff Saturday
    </a>
    ." He has played in 35 games for the Colts over 3 seasons.
</p>
