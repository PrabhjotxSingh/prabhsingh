<p>
    Troy was named All-GMC 1st team, All-Cincinnati 1st team, and All-Ohio 1st
    team as a defensive back while serving as a co-captain on Lakota's 1995
    OHSAA playoff team. In that same 1995 season, Troy set team receiving
    records and was also named All-GMC offensive 2nd team as a wide receiver.
    <br/>
    As a basketball player, Troy was named All-GMC 2nd team in 1995 and All-GMC
    1st team in 1996. He still holds the Lakota consecutive free throws made
    record of 33.
</p>
<p>
    Troy was inducted into the Lakota Hall of Fame in 2002.
</p>
<p>
    Troy was a four-year letterman and two-year starter at outside linebacker
    for the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cincinnati"
        title="University of Cincinnati"
    >
        University of Cincinnati
    </a>
    . As a senior, he was a second-team All-Conference USA selection and posted
    4.5 sacks and 107 tackles. As a junior, he made 96 tackles. He graduated
    from Cincinnati in 2000 with a business finance degree.
</p>
<p>
    Troy went on to play as an undrafted free agent for ten years in the NFL
    with Los Angeles (formerly St. Louis), Houston, and New Orleans. He was a
    team captain of the 2010 Super Bowl Champions, the New Orleans Saints.
</p>
<p>
    Following football, Troy founded MMT Properties, which buys and manages
    properties in the Metro Detroit area. In 2011, he created LBS Busing, which
    provides school bus transportation to communities that have failed levies
    or lost their busing programs. Troy is also a senior consultant at
    Healthcare Contracting Services. Troy was named in LEAD Cincinnati's Great
    Leaders Under 40's Class of 2015. He is currently the president of
    Medkinect, aspiring to transform the medical market.
</p>
