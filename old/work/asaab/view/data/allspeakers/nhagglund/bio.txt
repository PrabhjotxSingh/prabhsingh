
Lakota West Class of 2010: Soccer

<p>
    Nick Hagglund earned First Team All-GMC honors in soccer in 2008 and 2009.
    He was a Second Team All-State selection his senior year. Hagglund was
    named the team Offensive Player of the Year and MVP his senior year. He led
    his team to the regional finals his senior year.
</p>
<p>
    Hagglund played four years of
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/College_soccer"
        title="College soccer"
    >
        college soccer
    </a>
    at
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xavier_University"
        title="Xavier University"
    >
        Xavier University
    </a>
    . He made 81 appearances, starting in 80, while scoring four goals and
    adding 11 assists. Hagglund was named Big East Defensive Player of the Year
    for 2013, along with First Team All-Big East honors. During his collegiate
    career he has also earned A-10 Defensive Player honors and was named to the
    Atlantic 10 All-Conference First Team. In his freshman campaign he was
    named to Atlantic 10 All-Rookie Team and Atlantic 10 All-Tournament Team.
</p>
<p>
    Hagglund was drafted in the first round (10th overall) of the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_MLS_SuperDraft"
        title="2014 MLS SuperDraft"
    >
        2014 MLS SuperDraft
    </a>
    by
    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toronto_FC" title="Toronto FC">
        Toronto FC
    </a>
    . Hagglund made his professional debut in a 2-0 win over the
    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbus_Crew" title="Columbus Crew">
        Columbus Crew
    </a>
    on April 5, 2014. On November 30, 2016, Hagglund scored the goal that put
    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toronto_FC" title="Toronto FC">
        Toronto FC
    </a>
    ahead 3-2 against the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montreal_Impact"
        title="Montreal Impact"
    >
        Montreal Impact
    </a>
    in the Eastern Conference Final. This tied the aggregate score at 5-5, and
    sent the game into extra time. Toronto FC went on to score 2 in extra time
to win the game 5-2 and earn their first-ever appearance in the    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MLS_Cup" title="MLS Cup">MLS Cup</a>
    against the
    <a
        href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle_Sounders"
        title="Seattle Sounders"
    >
        Seattle Sounders
    </a>
    . Hagglund helped the team win the MLS Cup for Toronto in 2017.
</p>
<p>
    On January 23, 2019, Hagglund was traded to his hometown team
    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FC_Cincinnati" title="FC Cincinnati">
        FC Cincinnati
    </a>
    for their inaugural season in MLS. Cincinnati received the No. 24
    Allocation Ranking and Hagglund from Toronto in exchange for $200,000 in
    General Allocation Money in 2019, $100,000 in Targeted Allocation Money in
    2020 and the No. 1 Allocation Ranking. Hagglund started as a defender for
    FC Cincy in their first MLS game versus Seattle.
</p>
<p>
    Nick is married to a Lakota West grad, Mary, and they have a beautiful baby
    daughter, Lou.
</p>
